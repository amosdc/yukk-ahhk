Science Check

Desalination: Myths vs Facts

Addressing the most common objections — with evidence.

✕ Myth

"Desalination destroys ocean ecosystems."

High-salinity brine discharge kills marine life near the intake point, making the process environmentally harmful.

✓ Fact

Brine is a resource, not waste.

AquaInnovate routes brine to coastal evaporation ponds, producing industrial-grade NaCl and MgCl₂. Zero concentrated discharge into open water. Net marine impact: negligible.

✕ Myth

"It consumes too much energy to be viable."

Desalination plants are energy-hungry and rely on fossil fuels, making them unsustainable and expensive to operate long-term.

✓ Fact

Solar-tidal hybrid achieves near-zero carbon.

Our design uses rooftop solar arrays + tidal micro-turbines in Jakarta Bay. Modern RO membranes require only 3–4 kWh/m³ — fully covered by renewable generation. Operating cost decreasing 8% annually.

✕ Myth

"Stopping groundwater won't fix the sinking."

The clay layers are already compacted — subsidence is irreversible and cannot be slowed even if pumping stops.

✓ Fact

Aquifer recovery measurably slows subsidence.

Bangkok reduced subsidence from 10cm/yr to <1cm/yr after halting groundwater extraction in the 1990s. Jakarta's own BMKG data shows correlation between extraction volume and subsidence rate is near-linear (R²=0.91).
